# Cascade signals dilute abandonment — healthy transitive deps mask dead direct packages

## The Pattern\nRunning validation with --cascade drops exact match from 100% to 60%. Dead packages (pycrypto, nose, etc.) score HIGH instead of CRITICAL because their transitive dependencies are healthy packages, and the cascade signals contribute positive health.\n\n## Why It Matters\nA dead package with healthy transitive deps is STILL dead. The cascade health says nothing about the direct package's maintenance. Cascade should flag risk (unhealthy transitive deps) but should NOT improve the score of the root package.\n\n## Fix Direction\nCascade signals should be asymmetric: they can ADD risk (unhealthy transitive deps) but should NOT reduce risk (healthy transitive deps don't make a dead package safe). Only negative cascade signals should contribute to the score.
