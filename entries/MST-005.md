# Cascade collapse: adding randomness at stage 1 doesn't prevent determinism at stage 5

## Context

Working on persona-engine statistical analysis with Satish. Building a system that generates diverse outputs through a multi-stage pipeline.

## What Went Wrong

Added stochasticity (randomness) at the first stage of a multi-stage derivation pipeline, expecting output diversity at the end. The intermediate stages were all deterministic transforms. Result: outputs at stage 5 were nearly identical because the initial randomness got "absorbed" by the deterministic stages.

## Root Cause

A deterministic function of a random variable has lower entropy than the random variable itself. Each deterministic stage acts as a compression function, reducing the effective randomness. By stage 5, the initial randomness was gone.

## Prevention

Add residual stochasticity at EVERY derivation stage, not just the first. Each stage should introduce some independent randomness to prevent cascade collapse. This is analogous to residual connections in neural networks — they prevent signal degradation through deep layers.

The mathematical principle: entropy is non-increasing through deterministic transforms (data processing inequality). To maintain entropy, each stage must add new information.
