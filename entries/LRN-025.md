# Stale positive signals from old metadata inflate health scores on abandoned packages

## Context

During validation testing, the team found that packages known to be abandoned were receiving inappropriately high health scores, causing threshold mismatches in their risk assessment.

## What Happened

Packages like pycrypto and nose were showing HIGH risk (0.605) instead of the expected CRITICAL risk due to old positive signals. For example, a "Production/Stable" development status declaration from 2014 was being weighted the same as one from 2024.

## Insight

Positive signals from package metadata become misleading indicators of current health when they're significantly outdated. The age of the signal is as important as the signal itself for accurate risk assessment.

## Applies To

Any system that relies on historical metadata to assess current package health. Temporal weighting of signals is crucial for accurate abandonment detection.
