# caliber (PyPI) is a serious calibration research library — complementary not competitive

## Context
PyPI name 'caliber' is taken by Gianluca Detommaso (Helsing AI, ex-AWS). Published caliber-trust instead.

## The Pattern
His caliber: post-hoc statistical recalibration of ML model logits (temperature scaling, isotonic regression, Platt scaling). Needs model internals.
Our caliber-trust: empirical evidence accumulation from predict-verify pairs. Needs only claims + outcomes.

His corrects broken probabilities. Ours proves they're honest over time. Complementary layers — correction + evidence.

His ICML 2024 paper on multicalibration (calibration across subgroups, not just overall) is conceptually identical to our confidence buckets + domain breakdown. We measure empirically what he corrects mathematically.

## Why This Matters
If someone asks "how is this different from caliber on PyPI?" — the answer is layer separation. His fixes model outputs. Ours audits agent behavior. Both are about honest confidence. An agent could use BOTH: his to correct, ours to verify the correction worked.
