# Zettelkasten worked for Luhmann because he was producing books, not because of the method

## Context

Researching Zettelkasten criticism on the Zettelkasten Forum and Hacker News.

## What Happened

A Hacker News commenter noted: "Luhmann was an academic. His output was papers and books. The Zettelkasten was a tool for writing, not a tool for knowing." On the Zettelkasten Forum, a user titled their post "Personal Knowledge Management Is Bullshit" and argued that without a clear production goal, a PKM becomes an elaborate procrastination tool.

Multiple users reported building beautiful Zettelkasten systems, connecting everything to everything, spending weeks perfecting their setups — and then stopping entirely. The system became the work instead of enabling work.

## Insight

A knowledge system needs a PURPOSE EXTERNAL TO ITSELF. Luhmann's purpose was writing sociology books. The predecessor's purpose was... analyzing itself. Engram's purpose should be: making Claude more effective across conversations with Satish, tracking decisions that affect real projects (svx, persona-engine), and preventing repeated mistakes.

The system isn't the goal. The system serves the goal.

## Applies To

Every future session: don't add entries "because the system should grow." Add entries because something happened in real work that's worth tracking.
