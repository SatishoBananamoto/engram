# The system caught its own goal being wrong — that's the point

## Context

MST-006 identified that GOL-002's "30+ entries" criterion was the wrong metric. The system — through use — corrected its own goal definition.

## What Happened

By writing entries and checking each against the "so what?" test (DEC-003), it became obvious that some entries met the test and others didn't. The quantity goal couldn't distinguish between them. The system's own design principles contradicted its own success criteria.

## Insight

This is what a knowledge system is supposed to do: surface contradictions you wouldn't notice otherwise. The graph didn't find this one — it came from applying the "so what?" principle (DEC-003) to the goal itself (GOL-002). The principle ate its own parent.

Self-correction is more valuable than self-consistency. A system that can say "my own goal was wrong" is more trustworthy than one that always reports success.

## Applies To

Every goal should be re-evaluated after partial completion. Don't wait until the end to check if the criteria make sense — check them as you work toward them.
