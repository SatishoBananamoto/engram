# Stale positive signals are the root cause of score compression on dead packages

## Context\nVigil validation showed 6/20 exact mismatches. Two categories: dead packages scoring HIGH instead of CRITICAL, and healthy packages barely crossing the LOW/MODERATE boundary.\n\n## The Insight\nStale positive signals (dev_status='Production/Stable' from 2014, yanked_releases=0/13) were contributing health to dead packages. These signals are frozen metadata — they tell you what the package WAS, not what it IS. Reducing their confidence from 0.5/0.6 to 0.15/0.18 when last release > 2 years old pushed pycrypto/nose from 0.605 (HIGH) to 0.765 (CRITICAL).\n\n## Where It Applies\nAny scoring system that aggregates signals from different time periods. Signals from static metadata (classifiers, license, yank count) decay in value as the underlying project goes dormant. Activity signals (push recency, commit trend) don't have this problem because they naturally reflect current state.
