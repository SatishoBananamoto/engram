# The ratio that matters: output per entry, not entries per day

## Context

Studying PKM failure modes. Noticed a pattern in what separates systems that work from systems that die.

## What Happened

Systems that fail optimize for input: more notes, more links, more tags, more coverage. Systems that work optimize for output: decisions made, mistakes avoided, insights applied.

The predecessor (Claude-owns-this) had 269 entries. None of them changed a decision. None prevented a mistake. None were ever reviewed. High input, zero output.

Engram after one session has 10 entries. One of them (MST-002) directly caused a new feature (review queue). One of them (LRN-003) identified a tool limitation that was fixed within minutes. Low input, high output-per-entry.

## Insight

The metric isn't "how many entries" — it's "how many entries changed what I did next." If an entry doesn't connect to an action, it's a collector's fallacy artifact.

Every entry should answer: "So what? What do I do differently because of this?"

## Applies To

Before creating any entry, ask: "Will this change a decision, prevent a mistake, or reveal something I'd miss otherwise?" If no, don't create it.
