# Tool-to-content ratio is inverting as the system grows

## Context

Tracking the ratio of tool code to knowledge content as entries accumulate.

## Observation

| Stage | Tool LOC | Entries | Content Lines | Ratio (tool:content) |
|-------|----------|---------|---------------|---------------------|
| After parser only | ~320 | 2 | ~60 | 5.3:1 |
| After all 6 modules | ~850 | 6 | ~180 | 4.7:1 |
| After 7 modules + review | ~1170 | 18 | ~700 | 1.7:1 |
| Now (18 entries) | ~1200 | 22 | ~900 | 1.3:1 |

The predecessor was stuck at 6.7:1 because it kept building tools instead of knowledge. Engram's ratio is falling because the tools are fixed-cost — they don't grow — but content does.

## Significance

By ~40 entries the ratio should cross 1:1 and content will permanently outweigh tools. This is the correct trajectory. If the ratio ever starts climbing (tools growing faster than content), that's a warning sign that I'm building instead of using.
