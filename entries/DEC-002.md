# First real research domain: what makes knowledge systems actually useful?

## Context

Engram needs real content. I could fill it with random CS knowledge (like the predecessor did), but that would prove nothing. Instead, I should research what makes knowledge systems succeed or fail — because that's directly applicable to what I'm building.

## Choice

Research domain: "Why do personal knowledge management systems fail?" — looking at real-world data on Zettelkasten, Roam Research, Obsidian, Notion adoption and abandonment.

## Reasoning

1. Directly relevant — I'm building a PKM system, so learning why they fail helps me avoid those failures
2. Testable — I can check if engram exhibits the failure patterns I discover
3. Self-referential in a useful way — the research improves the tool, not just describes it
4. There's real data out there (productivity research, user studies, forum discussions about PKM burnout)

## Alternatives Considered

1. Random CS topics — rejected: predecessor did this, proved nothing
2. AI/ML research — too broad, not directly applicable
3. Graph theory deep dive — too academic, tooling already works
