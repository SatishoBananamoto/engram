# Every entry must pass the "so what?" test

## Context

Research on PKM failures (LRN-003) and the Collector's Fallacy (OBS-002) showed that most knowledge systems die because they accumulate information without connecting it to action.

## Choice

Before creating any entry, it must answer: "What do I do differently because of this?" If the answer is nothing, don't create the entry.

Entry types enforce this structurally:
- Decisions: "Alternatives Considered" proves you chose, not just noted
- Learnings: "Applies To" forces forward-looking connection
- Mistakes: "Prevention" requires an actionable fix
- Observations: "Significance" requires interpretation, not just data

## Reasoning

The predecessor had 269 entries. Zero of them changed a subsequent decision. Engram with 12 entries has already changed the tool (review queue added because of MST-002) and surfaced a design limitation (MST-003 led to integration tests).

Output per entry > entries per day.

## Alternatives Considered

1. No filter — just create entries freely. Rejected: leads to Collector's Fallacy.
2. Strict templates with mandatory sections. Rejected: too rigid, discourages capture.
3. "So what?" as a guideline, not a gate. Chosen: encourages quality without blocking speed.
