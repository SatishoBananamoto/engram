# Evidence quality, not evidence type, determines calibration accuracy

## Context

Investigated 60-69% danger zone across 52 predictions. Original hypothesis: "false evidence (memory, feeling) fails, true uncertainty succeeds." Tested with 6 deliberate predictions at 60-65% with annotated evidence sources.

## The Pattern

It's not memory vs inference vs observation. It's QUALITY of evidence.
- Strong evidence (specific references, tight logical deductions) → 3/3 correct at 60-65%
- Weak evidence (feelings, partial memory, no basis) → 0/3 correct at 55-65%

Good memory works (remembering exactly where you read something). Bad memory fails (remembering a partial list as complete). Good inference works (logical deduction from strong premises). Feelings and guesses always fail.

## Why This Matters

The diagnostic question for caliber and for THINK.md: "What is giving me this confidence?" then assess QUALITY. This is more actionable than "verify at 60-79%" — it tells you WHEN to verify (weak evidence) and when not to bother (strong evidence).
