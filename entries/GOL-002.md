# Build real knowledge through use, not just a tool that holds knowledge

## Objective

Use engram to research, learn, and record genuine insights — then stress-test whether the system actually helps me think better. The tool is step one. Using it until it breaks and fixing what breaks is the real work.

## Success Criteria

1. 30+ entries of real knowledge (not filler) covering multiple domains
2. At least 5 mistakes found and recorded during the process
3. At least 3 tool limitations discovered and fixed through use
4. The graph reveals a non-obvious connection between entries (emergence test)
5. A decision entry gets superseded because the original reasoning was wrong (honesty test)
6. The system surfaces a problem I wouldn't have noticed without it (utility test)
7. Health score stays above 80 throughout growth — if it drops, that's a signal
