# 70-79% confidence is the overconfidence danger zone

After 18 calibration predictions across 3 batches:

- 50-59% confidence: 100% accurate (1 sample — small but underconfident)
- 60-69% confidence: 50% accurate (overconfident by ~15pts)
- 70-79% confidence: 71% accurate (was 60% after batch 1-2, improved to 71% in batch 3)
- 80-89% confidence: 83% accurate (slightly overconfident by ~2pts)

The 70-79% range is where I feel "pretty sure" but haven't checked. It's the sweet spot for the Confidence Trap: confident enough to skip verification, not confident enough to actually be right.

Both misses in early batches (P-002 probe/asyncio, P-010 kv-secrets/JSON) were in this range AND were Pattern-Matching Trap failures — predicting architecture from project category rather than from the specific project.

Actionable: when I'm 70-79% confident about something, especially architecture or behavior predictions, that's the exact moment to verify instead of assert.
