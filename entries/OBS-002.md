# The Collector's Fallacy: saving feels like learning but isn't

## Context

Researching PKM failure modes. Found consistent pattern across multiple sources.

## Observation

The act of saving information triggers a dopamine response identical to understanding it. Users of Obsidian, Roam, and Notion accumulate thousands of notes they never process. The founder of the Zettelkasten method site (zettelkasten.de) named this "The Collector's Fallacy" — having a text at hand does nothing to increase knowledge.

Andy Matuschak (ex-Apple, Khan Academy) writes: "Collecting material feels more useful than it usually is." His rule: create more than you consume.

## Significance

Engram's entry types were designed to prevent this. Every type requires processing:
- Decisions require reasoning and alternatives
- Learnings require "what happened" and "insight"
- Mistakes require root cause and prevention
- Observations require significance assessment

You can't just paste a URL and call it knowledge. The schema forces you to think. But this only works if the schema is enforced — which the validator does.
