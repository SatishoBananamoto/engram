# Research domain should be "what I learn from real work", not "PKM theory"

## Context

DEC-002 chose "why PKM systems fail" as the first research domain. That research is done (LRN-003 through LRN-005, OBS-002 through OBS-004). But LRN-005 revealed something: the research about PKM systems was itself becoming a PKM exercise — studying knowledge management instead of managing knowledge about real work.

## Choice

Stop researching PKM theory. The failure modes are documented (LRN-003). Going deeper is the Collector's Fallacy in action. Future entries should come from actual work: svx development, persona-engine decisions, engineering problems Satish and I solve together.

## Reasoning

The Zettelkasten worked for Luhmann because his content was sociology, not "how Zettelkasten works." Engram works when its content is engineering decisions, not "how knowledge systems work." The meta-research served its purpose. Time to use the tool on real problems.

## Alternatives Considered

1. Continue PKM research deeper — rejected: diminishing returns, already have the key patterns
2. Switch to a different theoretical domain — rejected: same problem, just different topic
3. Stop adding entries until real work generates them — chosen: entries should come from work, not from reading about work
