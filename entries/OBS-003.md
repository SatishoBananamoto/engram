# Academic KM research shows knowledge APPLICATION matters most, not creation or sharing

## Context

Searched for longitudinal studies on knowledge management effectiveness.

## Observation

A 2025 systematic review (ScienceDirect) of KM success factors found: knowledge creation and capture had some impact on performance, knowledge sharing had nonsignificant effect, but knowledge APPLICATION had the strongest impact on operational performance.

Another finding: KM systems designed to foster learning can "paradoxically entrench outdated practices, creating competency traps." The system becomes a constraint instead of an enabler.

## Significance

This validates engram's design choices:
1. The review queue is an APPLICATION mechanism — it forces you to revisit and act on knowledge
2. The supersession system prevents competency traps — old decisions get explicitly replaced, not silently ignored
3. The "mistake" entry type is an application type — it changes future behavior

But it also reveals a gap: engram tracks what you know and what went wrong, but doesn't yet track what you DID with that knowledge. There's no "action" or "applied" entry type. Worth watching.
